First Nations Data Sovereignty

Technology guidance grounded in direct experience inside First Nations governance and infrastructure.

Data sovereignty means a Nation controls how its own information is collected, stored, used, and shared — rather than that control sitting with an outside government, company, or platform by default.

For First Nations, this extends the same principle of self-determination that already applies to land and governance into the digital systems Nations increasingly rely on: membership records, land and resource data, health and social information, and community communications.

MakwaByte works from direct experience inside First Nations governance and infrastructure roles, not as an outside vendor learning the context for the first time.

    Ownership stays with the Nation

    Systems are set up so the Nation holds and controls its own data.
  • 02
